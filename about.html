Abstract

Climate change is one of the most significant issues our planet has faced for years. With the world's rapid development, the threat of climate change grows considerably. When hazardous gases such as sulphur dioxide (SO2) and nitrogen oxides (NOx) are combined with rain, the acidity of the precipitation increases. When this acidic rain falls, major issues ensue. Carbon dioxide (CO2) is produced when acids reacts with calcium carbonate (CaCO3), the primary component of limestone. This process is simulated by our prototype. The limestone is placed in a container, and the acidic water, whose pH was previously determined, is poured over it. After allowing the mixture to react, the MQ-135 sensor, which measures in parts per million (ppm), will measure the CO2 concentration. The specified design requirements are the pH measuring system's accuracy and a Graphical User Interface (GUI) that displays all the data necessary for the user to interpret the readings. To test the design requirements, four acidic samples, each with a specific pH value, were used in four separate tests. These procedures led us to the conclusion that as the acidity of the rain grows, so does the level of CO2 in the atmosphere, causing major problems such as global warming and a number of adverse health effects.
